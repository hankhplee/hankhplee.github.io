I am an incoming Ph.D. student in the Human-Computer Interaction Institute at Carnegie Mellon University, advised by Dr. Sauvik Das and Dr. Jodi Forlizzi. I study how AI practitioners approach privacy considerations when creating AI products, and develop artifacts and design methods to support such processes.
I earned my B.S. in Computer Science at National Chiao Tung University, where I worked with Dr. Yung-Ju (Stanley) Chang. I was a Visiting Researcher at the University of Melbourne, where I worked with Dr. Tilman Dingler, and was a visiting student at National Taiwan University, where I worked with Dr. Shang-Tse Chen.
Research Interests: Human-Computer Interaction, Ubiquitous Computing, Usable Privacy & Security, Computer-Mediated Communication
